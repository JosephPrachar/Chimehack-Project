What is sexual assault?

The United States Department of Justice: "Sexual assault is any type of sexual contact or behavior that occurs without the explicit consent of the recipient. Falling under the definition of sexual assault are sexual activities as forced sexual intercourse, forcible sodomy, child molestation, incest, fondling, and attempted rape."

Some examples of the many forms that sexual assault can take:
  1. Rape
  2. Attempted rape
  3. Sexual coercion
  4. Unwanted fondling
  5. Voyeurism
  6. Sexual harassment or threats

Write
Writing down sexual assault tetimonials and having everything written down in one place can make victims feel more comfortable and relaxed. This record can be used if the victim every chooses to press charges.

Recover
Reliving experiences of sexual assault can be tramatic and emotional experiences. We understand that coming forward can take time and victims might not neccesarily be ready right away.

Report
When or if a victim is ready to report the assault, their record of events will have saved all the details of the assault. A victims testemonial will have validity if they have a record of the assault closer to the time it occured.
