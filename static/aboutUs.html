About Us

Why We Do What We Do

Women cannot easily report sexual assault. Pushing past the insecurities and finding the strength to report an incident is the first hurdle. Finding the resources to report a sexual assault incident is an even more difficult task. We want to produce a way for victims of sexual assault to keep a record of the incident (write), provide support for them (recover), and supply an easy, streamlined way for them to find help (report).

Statistics

To show the extent of sexual assault. Help us help others from becoming another statistic.

One in 5 women and one in 13 men will be sexually assaulted during their college career in the United States.
Less than 10% will ever report their assault to their school or to the police.
On average, those who do report wait 11 months.
90% of sexual assaults are committed by repeat offenders.
Only 6% of sexual assaults reported to police end with the assailant spending a single day in prison7, meaning that there's a 99% chance that they'll get away with it. This means there is practically no deterrent to sexual assault in the US.
We could prevent 59% of assaults from ever occurring - just by halting repeat offenders earlier on.
